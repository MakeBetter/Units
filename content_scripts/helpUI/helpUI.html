Browse the web like a boss! Here's a few tips to get you started...

Easy Navigation

  1. Visit one of: Google Search, Facebook, Twitter or Stack Overflow.
  2. Notice that the first main unit on the page is selected. Press j and k to move between the units.
  3. In most cases, the most important link in the unit is already selected for you. Press o to open it in a new tab or enter to open in the the same tab.
  4. Use tab to move focus to the next link in the page.
    5. 'j' and 'k' are "smart scrolling" keys. On a page with units, it smartly decides between when to select the next unit and when to scroll the page. For pages with no units, 'j' and 'k' will simply smooth scroll the page.

Other useful features:

  1. Spotlight mode: This one is great for focused reading. Increase spotlight on the selected unit by pressing "+" and decrease with "-".
  2. Filter Units: You can filter units based on a search term. It hides all the units from the page that don't match. Shortcut: "alt+f"

Disable and configure:

  1. To quickly disable Units on any page, press ",".
    To disable Units permanently on a site: Click on the Units extension icon in the browser and disable from there.
  2. Units is highly configurable. See the Options page to change settings and shortcuts.

What is a Content Unit?

A Content Unit represents a logical unit of content on a webpage. :P Search results pages, social network feeds, Q&A sites, news sites and blogs: all commonly present the primary content on the page as distinct 'units' or blocks. We call these blocks 'Content Units'.
