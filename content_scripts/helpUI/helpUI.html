Browse the web like a boss! Here's a few tips to get you started...

Pages with 'CUs':

  1. Visit one of: Google Search, Facebook, Twitter or Stack Overflow.
  2. Notice that the first content unit (CU) on the page is selected. Press j and k to move between CUs.
  3. In most cases, the most important link in the unit is already selected for you. Press o to open it in a new tab or enter to open in the the same tab.
  4. Use tab to move focus to the next link in the page.

All pages:

Give these general utilities a spin. We think these are essential for a seamless 'unit-y' experience.

  1. On pages with no CUs, use j and k to scroll the page. (Marvel at how smooth it is :)
  2. Protip: Use and to move between tabs.
  3. Quickly access any link on the page. Press space and letter to reach a link starting with that letter.
  4. Press z to try out the Zen Mode (on pages that support it).

This is a good start. But there's lots more. Do explore the shortcuts in detail and our website if you like it thus far.

Also note Units is highly configurable. Check out the Options page for more.

What is a Content Unit?

A Content Unit represents a logical unit of content on a webpage. :P Search results pages, social network feeds, Q&A sites, news sites and blogs: all commonly present the primary content on the page as distinct 'units' or blocks. We call these blocks 'Content Units'.
